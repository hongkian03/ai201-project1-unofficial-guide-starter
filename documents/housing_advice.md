Oh baby.... I am so ready to answer this.

- You are going to live in Warren Towers, West Campus, or Fenway Campus. You're being way way too picky lmao.

Warren is the best place to make friends but is dated and has no AC+ has communal bathrooms, West is a great place to make friends and is more modern than warren but a bit far from campus and has no AC+has communal bathrooms. Fenway campus is far from campus and has a range of rooms.

Lower your expectations. You will be living with no AC and using communal bathrooms your freshman year. Sorry!

- For the spots you are curious about

- Bay State Road (Brownstones):Yeah they're kinda old but in a quaint way. The location is top tier and the street is beautiful, plus having to share a bathroom with only 6-8 people is a huge plus. You might be able to get one sophomore or junior year.
- 610 Beacon Street: One of the nicest dorms on campus. Formerly called Myles. They are in suites, meaning you are living in a "cluster" of rooms with a common room and a bathroom instead of living in a hallway with a communal bathroom. You might get to live in 610 your senior year or junior year. This is super competitive!
- Apartments: All apartments are extremely competitive. Even the tiny basement apartments are hard to get. You might get one as a senior. No chance as a freshman, sophomore, and very slim as a junior.
- Kilachand: If you are a KHC student you can live there. The downside is that you will most likely be in a triple occupancy room.

- Are there any large pros/cons between West, Central, East, South Campus, and Student Village? Yeah it's how far away from classes they are baby girl! West has the gym, band rooms, and is close to CFA and CGS. Central is in the middle of everything and is super convenient. East is also super convenient, but too far east at like danielsen is not convenient. South is for seniors and juniors. Fenway is super nice but is far. Stuvi (student village) is in west.
- Look into Hojo, might be a good option. Has AC, private bathrooms, and nice location, but you will likely be in a triple. Also, look into Riverway house. Has AC, private bathrooms, but the location is far. These are obtainable options for first years!

Anyway, lmk if you have any questions. BU housing is literally my hyperfixation and I know everything because I am very smart and intelligent and humble.
