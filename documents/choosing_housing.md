There are just as many first years in West as in Warren. Some say it's harder to make friends in West because there aren't common rooms in each floor but you just need to make an effort to meet people in your floor or hang out in the Rich Hall common areas on the first floor.

This is the first major renovation of Warren since it was built. One tower will be shutdown. I hope they offer a discounted rate for those living in the other 2 towers of Warren because the construction is probably going to be LOUD. Back when they renovated 610 Beacon, they offered a discount to those living in that building while half of it was under construction. There will be less students there while 1 tower is shutdown each year for renovations. But it'll still be busy and all on campus students with a dining plan have access so it'll still be a thriving space to meet people and hang out.

West still has a lot of heart. It's very popular with the athletes. The views of Nickerson Field from half the rooms is nice but be prepared for bright lights and music well into the late night due to team practices. Close to the gym too and some take that as motivation to go more often. Pro tip: go to your college gym. This is like the only time you have access to such a nice facility while it's "free" (included in tuition for full time students).

Usually the college/program you're in doesn't dictate where your classes take place but QST (BU loves their acronyms, so start getting used to it 😅) is the exception. Most of your classes will probably be in the QST/HAR building, 595 Comm Ave. If you want to be close to your classes then you probably will want to be in Warren or The Towers. Note there is a difference between Warren towers and The Towers. We just called Warren Warren and the towers people call Towers. Towers is all single sex floors because there's only one shared bathroom. It's a little quieter than Warren and West historically and you might have some more sophomores living there but it's still a lot of freshmen there. It's on baystate rather than, so it's a quieter area no dining hall inside the building though. Physically closer to QST though.

Housing selection for first years is the illusion of choice. BU will put you wherever there are vacancies with most freshmen being put in West Warren and Towers. I did get my first choice of West Campus when I was a first year. I did not select all female floor but because there's just more women at BU I was put in in all female floor. The ranked selection of dorms you give in the housing portal usually means nothing. People will even specifically request a specific roommate and like both roommates will request each other and housing doesn't always grant that. Hypothetically it should be fine because all the first years are being assigned housing at the same time just put these two humans together if they want the same housing, but people find all the time that the roommate they mutually selected they didn't get during housing assignment.
